> Oil was cheap; ash was free. The gatekeeper had learned long ago that the
> things which cost nothing were the hardest to be rid of.

The second chapter shifts perspective. Below, a note on a recurring metaphor.

## Translator's note

The novel repeatedly pairs _ash_ (what remains) with _oil_ (what is spent). In
the source language these share a root that English lacks. I chose to keep the
pairing visible by always placing the two words close together, trusting the
reader to feel the rhyme of meaning even without the rhyme of sound.
