## Overview

The MITRE Embedded Capture-the-Flag (eCTF) drops you into a deliberately
vulnerable embedded system and asks you to secure it against a live attack
scenario. Our work focused on the firmware.

## What I did

- **Rewrote vulnerable firmware routines in C**, removing the memory-safety bugs
  that made exploitation possible in the first place.
- Enabled **stack canaries** and **MPU memory protections**, closing
  buffer-overflow and unauthorized-flash-access vulnerabilities in the
  competition scenario.

## What I learned

On constrained hardware, security is a budget: every protection costs cycles and
flash. The exercise was as much about _which_ defenses to spend on for a given
threat model as it was about implementing them.
